## 1. INTRODUCTION AND PURPOSE

### 1.1 Purpose

This Anti-Money Laundering and Countering the Financing of Terrorism ("AML/CFT") Compliance Manual sets out the policies, procedures, and internal controls adopted by Pefko Capital Management Pte. Ltd. (the "Company") to prevent the Company from being used, intentionally or unintentionally, as a conduit for money laundering ("ML"), terrorism financing ("TF"), or proliferation financing ("PF").

### 1.2 Scope

This Manual applies to all directors, officers, employees, and representatives of the Company. It covers all business relationships and transactions conducted by the Company, including the management of the Pefko Fund and interactions with investors (limited partners), portfolio companies, counterparties, and service providers.

### 1.3 Policy Statement

The Company is committed to:

(a) Complying with all applicable AML/CFT laws, regulations, notices, and guidelines, including MAS Notice SFA 04-N02 and its accompanying guidelines;

(b) Identifying, assessing, and mitigating ML/TF/PF risks in its business activities;

(c) Implementing robust customer due diligence procedures;

(d) Monitoring business relationships and transactions for suspicious activities;

(e) Reporting suspicious transactions to the Suspicious Transaction Reporting Office ("STRO") promptly;

(f) Maintaining comprehensive records in accordance with regulatory requirements; and

(g) Ensuring all staff receive adequate AML/CFT training.

### 1.4 Consequences of Non-Compliance

Non-compliance with AML/CFT requirements may result in:
- Criminal prosecution under the Corruption, Drug Trafficking and Other Serious Crimes (Confiscation of Benefits) Act ("CDSA"), the Terrorism (Suppression of Financing) Act ("TSOFA"), or the SFA
- Regulatory action by MAS, including fines, license revocation, or restrictions on business
- Reputational damage to the Company
- Disciplinary action against responsible individuals

### 2.1 Applicable Laws and Regulations

The Company's AML/CFT program is designed to comply with:

| Legislation/Regulation | Description |
|------------------------|-------------|
| Securities and Futures Act 2001 ("SFA") | Licensing and conduct requirements for capital markets intermediaries |
| Financial Services and Markets Act 2022 ("FSM Act") | MAS regulatory powers |

### 2.2 VCFM-Specific Considerations

As a VCFM, the Company manages funds offered exclusively to accredited and institutional investors. While this presents a lower ML/TF risk profile compared to retail-facing financial institutions, the Company nevertheless maintains robust AML/CFT controls appropriate to its risk profile.

The Company's primary AML/CFT risks relate to:
- Investor (LP) onboarding — ensuring legitimate source of funds and beneficial ownership
- Cross-border capital flows — fund commitments from multiple jurisdictions
- Portfolio company investments — ensuring no connection to sanctioned persons or entities

## 4. ENTERPRISE-WIDE RISK ASSESSMENT

### 4.1 Risk Assessment Methodology

The Company conducts an enterprise-wide ML/TF risk assessment at least annually, or when there is a material change in the Company's business, products, or customer base. The risk assessment considers:

(a) **Customer risk** — types of investors, their jurisdictions, business activities, and source of funds/wealth;

(b) **Country/geographic risk** — jurisdictions of investors and portfolio companies, with reference to FATF high-risk and grey-list jurisdictions;

(c) **Product/service risk** — nature of fund management activities, fund structure, and investment strategy;

(d) **Channel/delivery risk** — methods of investor onboarding and capital movement; and

(e) **Other risk factors** — emerging typologies, regulatory guidance, and industry intelligence.

### 4.2 Risk Ratings

Each risk factor is assessed and rated as:

| Rating | Description | Action Required |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Low** | ML/TF risk is low based on available information | Standard CDD; routine monitoring |
| **Medium** | Some risk factors present that warrant attention | Standard CDD with additional measures; enhanced monitoring |
| **High** | Significant ML/TF risk factors present | Enhanced Due Diligence; senior management approval; active monitoring |

### 4.3 Current Risk Assessment Summary

| Risk Category | Current Rating | Key Factors |
|---------------|---------------|-------------|
| **Customer Risk** | Medium | Institutional/accredited investors; multi-jurisdictional LP base; some investors from higher-risk jurisdictions (China ODI pathway) |
| **Country Risk** | Medium | Investors from Singapore, China, and potentially other jurisdictions; Cayman fund domicile |
| **Product Risk** | Low | Closed-end VC fund; illiquid investments; no continuous subscription/redemption |
| **Channel Risk** | Low | Direct relationships with institutional investors; no intermediary distribution |
| **Overall** | **Medium** | Appropriate for a VC fund manager with cross-border investor base |

### 4.4 Risk Mitigation Measures

Based on the risk assessment, the Company has implemented:

- Standard CDD for all investors at onboarding
- Enhanced CDD for investors from higher-risk jurisdictions or with complex ownership structures
- Sanctions screening against all applicable lists at onboarding and on an ongoing basis
- Source of funds/wealth verification for all investors
- Ongoing monitoring of investor relationships and transactions
- Regular staff training on ML/TF risks and typologies

## 5. CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE (CDD)

### 5.1 When CDD Must Be Performed

CDD must be performed:

(a) Before establishing a business relationship with a new investor (LP);

(b) Before carrying out any transaction for a customer with whom the Company has no existing business relationship;

(c) When there is suspicion of ML/TF, regardless of any exemption or threshold;

(d) When there is doubt about the veracity or adequacy of previously obtained CDD information; and

(e) Periodically during the relationship, in accordance with the customer's risk rating.

### 5.2 No Anonymous Accounts

The Company shall not establish or maintain an anonymous account or an account in a fictitious name for any investor.

### 5.3 CDD Requirements — Individual Investors

For individual investors (natural persons), the Company shall obtain and verify:

(a) **Full name** (as per official identification document)
(b) **Date of birth**
(c) **Nationality**
(d) **Residential address**
(e) **Unique identification number** (NRIC, FIN, or Passport number)
(f) **Source of funds** (for the investment commitment)
(g) **Source of wealth** (overall)
(h) **Purpose of the investment**
(i) **Occupation / business activities**

**Verification documents:**
- Certified copy of NRIC, passport, or equivalent government-issued photo identification
- Proof of residential address (utility bill, bank statement, or government correspondence dated within 3 months)
- Supporting documentation for source of funds/wealth

### 5.4 CDD Requirements — Corporate / Institutional Investors

For corporate or institutional investors, the Company shall obtain and verify:

(a) **Full legal name and registration number**
(b) **Country and date of incorporation/establishment**
(c) **Registered address and principal place of business**
(d) **Nature of business**
(e) **Ownership and control structure** (including identification of all beneficial owners holding ≥25% or, where no such person exists, the senior managing official)
(f) **Identity of directors**
(g) **Identity of authorised signatories**
(h) **Source of funds** (for the investment commitment)
(i) **Purpose of the investment**

**Verification documents:**
- Certificate of incorporation or equivalent
- Memorandum/Articles of Association or equivalent constitutional documents
- Register of directors and shareholders (or equivalent)
- Board resolution or authorisation for the investment
- Proof of registered address
- Identification documents for beneficial owners (as per individual CDD)

### 5.5 Beneficial Ownership

The Company shall identify the beneficial owners of every investor entity. Beneficial owners are:

(a) Natural persons who ultimately own or control, directly or indirectly, 25% or more of the shares or voting rights of the investor entity;

(b) Natural persons who exercise ultimate effective control over the investor entity; or

(c) Where no such natural person can be identified, the natural person holding the position of senior managing official.

The Company shall take reasonable steps to verify the identity of beneficial owners using reliable and independent sources.

### 5.6 Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)

The Company shall determine whether any investor, beneficial owner, or connected person is a Politically Exposed Person ("PEP"), defined as an individual who is or has been entrusted with prominent public functions.

If a PEP is identified:
- Enhanced Due Diligence must be performed (see Section 6)
- Senior management approval must be obtained before establishing or continuing the relationship
- Enhanced ongoing monitoring must be applied

### 5.7 Timing of CDD

CDD must be completed before establishing the business relationship (i.e., before accepting the investor's subscription to the Fund). Where it is not possible to complete CDD before establishing the relationship due to operational necessity:

(a) CDD must be completed as soon as reasonably practicable;
(b) The ML/TF risk arising from the delayed verification must be effectively managed; and
(c) No capital calls or distributions shall be processed until CDD is completed.

### 5.8 Failure to Complete CDD

If the Company is unable to complete CDD to its satisfaction, it shall:

(a) Not establish the business relationship or carry out the transaction;
(b) Consider filing an STR with STRO if there are grounds for suspicion; and
(c) Terminate any existing relationship if adequate CDD cannot be maintained.

## 6. ENHANCED DUE DILIGENCE (EDD)

### 6.1 When EDD Is Required

Enhanced Due Diligence must be performed in the following circumstances:

(a) The investor or beneficial owner is a PEP, or a family member or close associate of a PEP;

(b) The investor is from or has substantial business activities in a country identified by FATF as having strategic AML/CFT deficiencies (high-risk or grey-listed);

(c) The investor has a complex or opaque ownership structure with no apparent legitimate purpose;

(d) The investor's source of funds or source of wealth cannot be readily established;

(e) The enterprise-wide risk assessment identifies the relationship as higher-risk; or

(f) Any other circumstance that presents a higher ML/TF risk.

### 6.2 EDD Measures

In addition to standard CDD, EDD measures include:

(a) **Senior management approval** for establishing or continuing the business relationship;

(b) **Enhanced source of funds/wealth investigation** — obtaining additional documentation and conducting independent verification of the source of the investor's funds and wealth;

(c) **Purpose of relationship** — obtaining additional information on the reasons for the intended investment;

(d) **Adverse media screening** — conducting comprehensive adverse media searches on the investor, beneficial owners, and key connected persons;

(e) **Enhanced ongoing monitoring** — applying more frequent and intensive transaction monitoring to the relationship;

(f) **Additional documentation** — obtaining third-party verification, legal opinions, or other supporting documentation as appropriate; and

(g) **Regulatory checks** — checking relevant regulatory databases and public records.

### 6.3 Higher-Risk Countries

The Company maintains and regularly updates a list of higher-risk countries based on:
- FATF high-risk jurisdictions (call for action)
- FATF grey list (increased monitoring)
- MAS directives and advisories
- Internal risk assessment

Investors from higher-risk countries require EDD regardless of other risk factors.

## 7. SIMPLIFIED DUE DILIGENCE (SDD)

### 7.1 When SDD May Be Applied

Simplified Due Diligence may be applied in limited circumstances where the investor presents demonstrably lower ML/TF risk:

(a) The investor is a financial institution regulated by MAS or an equivalent foreign regulatory authority that is subject to and supervised for AML/CFT compliance consistent with FATF standards;

(b) The investor is a Singapore government entity or statutory body;

(c) The investor is a listed company on a recognised securities exchange subject to regulatory disclosure requirements; or

(d) The investor falls within another category approved by the Compliance Officer as presenting low ML/TF risk.

### 7.2 SDD Measures

Simplified measures may include:
- Verification of the investor's identity through publicly available information
- Reliance on the investor's own AML/CFT compliance program
- Reduced frequency of periodic CDD reviews

SDD does not exempt the Company from:
- Identifying the investor and verifying its identity
- Ongoing monitoring of the relationship
- Filing STRs when suspicious activity is identified

## 8. ONGOING MONITORING

### 8.1 Transaction Monitoring

The Company shall monitor all transactions throughout the business relationship to:

(a) Ensure transactions are consistent with the Company's knowledge of the investor, its business, and risk profile;

(b) Identify unusual or suspicious transactions, including complex, unusually large, or unusual patterns of transactions that have no apparent or visible economic or lawful purpose; and

(c) Ensure CDD information is kept up to date.

### 8.2 Key Monitoring Activities

| Activity | Frequency | Responsible |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Capital call/distribution review | Per event | Investment Team + Compliance |
| Sanctions screening (investor base) | At least quarterly | Compliance Officer |
| Adverse media monitoring | At least quarterly | Compliance Officer |
| CDD review — high-risk investors | Annually | Compliance Officer |
| CDD review — medium-risk investors | Every 2 years | Compliance Officer |
| CDD review — low-risk investors | Every 3 years | Compliance Officer |
| Investor profile change review | Per event (material changes) | Investment Team + Compliance |

### 8.3 Red Flags

The Company shall pay special attention to the following red flags:

(a) Investor requests to make or receive capital contributions using cash or bearer instruments;
(b) Capital contributions from unrelated third parties without adequate explanation;
(c) Requests to transfer LP interests to unknown or unrelated parties;
(d) Reluctance to provide CDD information or source of funds documentation;
(e) Investors from FATF high-risk or grey-list jurisdictions with unusual transaction patterns;
(f) Adverse media reports concerning an investor or its beneficial owners;
(g) Investor structures that appear designed to obscure beneficial ownership;
(h) Requests for unusual fund distribution arrangements;
(i) Frequent or unexplained changes in investor contact information or ownership;
(j) Any transaction or pattern of transactions that appears inconsistent with the investor's stated purpose or profile.

## 9. SUSPICIOUS TRANSACTION REPORTING (STR)

### 9.1 Obligation to Report

The Company is required to file a Suspicious Transaction Report ("STR") with the Suspicious Transaction Reporting Office ("STRO") when:

(a) There are reasonable grounds to suspect that any property is connected to drug dealing or criminal conduct under the CDSA; or

(b) There are reasonable grounds to suspect that any transaction or proposed transaction may involve terrorism financing under TSOFA.

### 9.2 Reporting Procedures

1. **Detection:** Any employee who identifies a suspicious transaction or activity must immediately report it to the Compliance Officer.

2. **Assessment:** The Compliance Officer shall assess the report, obtain additional information if necessary, and determine whether there are reasonable grounds to file an STR.

3. **Filing:** If the Compliance Officer determines that an STR is warranted, the STR shall be filed with STRO electronically via the STRO Online Notices and Reporting Platform ("SONAR") as soon as reasonably practicable and in any event within the timeframe prescribed by STRO.

4. **Record:** A copy of the STR and all supporting documentation shall be maintained in a secure file accessible only to the Compliance Officer and authorised persons.

5. **Escalation:** Material STRs shall be reported to the CEO and Board of Directors.

### 9.3 Tipping Off

No director, officer, employee, or representative of the Company shall disclose ("tip off") to any customer or third party:

(a) That an STR has been or will be filed;
(b) The contents of any STR;
(c) That any investigation is being or may be conducted in relation to ML/TF; or
(d) Any other information that may prejudice an ML/TF investigation.

Tipping off is a criminal offence under the CDSA and TSOFA.

### 9.4 Protection for Reporters

No person shall be subject to any civil, criminal, or disciplinary action for making a bona fide report of suspicious activity. The Company shall protect the identity of persons who file internal suspicious activity reports.

## 10. SANCTIONS SCREENING

### 10.1 Applicable Sanctions Lists

The Company shall screen all investors, beneficial owners, directors, and relevant connected persons against:

(a) **MAS Sanctions Lists** — lists issued pursuant to MAS regulations and directions;
(b) **United Nations Security Council Consolidated List** — as implemented in Singapore under the United Nations Act;
(c) **Singapore Monetary Authority of Singapore Directives** — specific designations and restrictions;
(d) **OFAC SDN List** (US) — given the Fund's USD denomination and potential US nexus; and
(e) **EU Sanctions List** — as appropriate based on investor/portfolio company jurisdictions.

### 10.2 Screening Procedures

| Action | When | Responsible |
|--------|------|-------------|
| New investor screening | Before accepting subscription | Compliance Officer |
| Existing investor re-screening | At least quarterly | Compliance Officer |
| Portfolio company screening | Before investment | Investment Team + Compliance |
| Ad hoc screening | Upon regulatory update or alert | Compliance Officer |

### 10.3 Sanctions Match Procedures

If a potential match is identified:

1. Conduct further investigation to confirm or dismiss the match (false positive analysis)
2. If confirmed, immediately freeze the relevant account/assets
3. Report to MAS and relevant authorities as required by law
4. Do not process any transaction involving the sanctioned party
5. Document the investigation and outcome

## 11. RECORD KEEPING

### 11.1 Records to be Maintained

The Company shall maintain the following records:

(a) **CDD records** — all information and documents obtained during CDD (including copies of identification documents, verification records, risk assessments);
(b) **Transaction records** — records of all transactions, including the nature, date, and amount;
(c) **STR records** — copies of all STRs filed and supporting documentation;
(d) **Internal reports** — records of internal suspicious activity reports;
(e) **Sanctions screening records** — results of all sanctions screenings;
(f) **Training records** — records of AML/CFT training attended by staff;
(g) **Risk assessment records** — enterprise-wide risk assessment and customer risk assessment documentation; and
(h) **Correspondence** — relevant correspondence with customers, MAS, STRO, and law enforcement.

### 11.2 Retention Period

All AML/CFT records shall be retained for at least **5 years** from:

(a) The date the business relationship is terminated (for CDD and relationship records); or
(b) The date the transaction is completed (for transaction records).

Records shall be retained for longer periods if:
- Required by MAS, STRO, or law enforcement; or
- Relating to a matter under investigation or that has been the subject of an STR.

### 11.3 Record Storage

Records shall be maintained in a secure manner (physical and/or electronic) with appropriate access controls. Records must be readily retrievable and available for inspection by MAS or other competent authorities.

## 13. INDEPENDENT AUDIT

### 13.1 Scope

The Company shall arrange for an independent audit of its AML/CFT policies, procedures, and controls:

(a) At least once every 3 years; or
(b) More frequently if directed by MAS or if material changes to the business warrant it.

### 13.2 Audit Coverage

The independent audit shall assess:

(a) Adequacy and effectiveness of the Company's AML/CFT framework;
(b) Compliance with MAS Notice SFA 04-N02 and applicable guidelines;
(c) Quality of CDD and ongoing monitoring procedures;
(d) Timeliness and quality of STR filings;
(e) Adequacy of sanctions screening procedures;
(f) Quality and completeness of record keeping; and
(g) Effectiveness of staff training.

### 13.3 Audit Findings

Audit findings shall be reported to the Board of Directors. Management shall prepare a remediation plan for any deficiencies identified, with timelines for implementation.

## 14. RELIANCE ON THIRD PARTIES

### 14.1 Conditions for Reliance

The Company may rely on third parties (including fund administrators, correspondent financial institutions, or other regulated entities) to perform CDD on its behalf, provided that:

(a) The third party is a financial institution subject to and supervised by a regulatory authority for AML/CFT compliance consistent with FATF standards;

(b) The Company obtains from the third party all CDD information and documentation immediately upon request;

(c) The Company is satisfied that the third party's CDD procedures are adequate and meet the Company's requirements;

(d) The Company retains ultimate responsibility for CDD compliance — reliance on a third party does not relieve the Company of its obligations under MAS Notice SFA 04-N02.

### 14.2 Fund Administrator

Where the Company engages a fund administrator to perform investor onboarding and CDD:

(a) The fund administrator must be a regulated entity with robust AML/CFT procedures;
(b) The Company shall conduct due diligence on the fund administrator's AML/CFT capabilities;
(c) The engagement terms shall specify AML/CFT obligations and the Company's right to access all CDD records; and
(d) The Compliance Officer shall periodically review the fund administrator's CDD work for quality and completeness.

## 15. WIRE TRANSFERS AND CORRESPONDENT RELATIONSHIPS

### 15.1 Capital Call and Distribution Payments

All capital calls and distributions shall be made by wire transfer to/from bank accounts in the name of the investor (or the Fund, as applicable). The Company shall:

(a) Not accept capital contributions in cash, bearer instruments, or from unverified sources;
(b) Verify that the remitting/receiving bank account corresponds to the investor's CDD records;
(c) Investigate any discrepancies or unusual payment patterns; and
(d) Maintain records of all fund flows.

### 15.2 Correspondent Relationships

The Company shall not enter into or maintain a correspondent relationship with a shell financial institution or an institution that permits its accounts to be used by shell financial institutions.

## 16. NEW PRODUCTS AND TECHNOLOGIES

### 16.1 ML/TF Risk Assessment for New Activities

Before launching a new fund, adopting a new technology platform, or entering a new market or jurisdiction, the Company shall:

(a) Assess the ML/TF risks associated with the new activity;
(b) Implement appropriate controls to mitigate identified risks; and
(c) Obtain Compliance Officer and Board approval.

### 16.2 Technology-Enabled Services

Where the Company uses technology-enabled services (e.g., digital investor onboarding, electronic document management), it shall ensure that:

(a) The technology does not compromise the quality of CDD;
(b) Appropriate safeguards are in place to prevent misuse; and
(c) The technology meets MAS requirements for electronic verification.
