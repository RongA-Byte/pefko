## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Pefko Capital Management Pte. Ltd. (the "Company") is a Singapore-incorporated private limited company established to manage the Pefko deep-technology venture capital fund. The Company seeks a Capital Markets Services License under the Venture Capital Fund Manager ("VCFM") regime.

**Key highlights:**

- **Strategy:** Deep-technology venture capital focused on early-stage and growth-stage investments in AI/ML, advanced computing, semiconductor design, quantum technologies, robotics, advanced materials, and cleantech
- **Fund:** Pefko (Cayman Exempted Limited Partnership), targeting US$75 million (range US$50M–$100M)
- **Investors:** Accredited and institutional investors only; not continuously offered or redeemable
- **VCFM eligibility:** ≥80% of committed capital invested in securities of unlisted ventures incorporated ≤10 years
- **Team:** Singapore-based management with minimum 2 Responsible Officers
- **Office:** Dedicated physical office in Singapore

The Company's business model is straightforward: manage a single closed-end venture capital fund investing in deep-technology ventures. The Company does not intend to manage multiple funds simultaneously during the initial licensing period and will focus exclusively on establishing and deploying the Pefko Fund.

## 3. INVESTMENT STRATEGY

### 3.1 Investment Thesis

The Company's investment thesis is based on the following core beliefs:

1. **Deep-tech is entering a commercialisation inflection point.** Advances in AI, computing hardware, and synthetic biology are transitioning from laboratory stage to commercial application, creating significant venture investment opportunities.

2. **Asia-Pacific is a critical hub for deep-tech development.** Singapore, China, Japan, South Korea, and India have strong engineering talent pools, government R&D investment, and growing startup ecosystems in deep technology.

3. **Early-stage deep-tech requires patient, specialist capital.** Deep-tech ventures have longer development cycles than software companies and require investors who understand the technology, can assess technical risk, and can provide strategic support during commercialisation.

4. **Venture-scale returns are achievable in deep-tech.** The high barriers to entry, significant IP moats, and large addressable markets in deep-tech sectors can generate outsized venture returns for well-selected portfolios.

### 3.2 Target Sectors

| Sector | Sub-Sectors | Rationale |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **AI/ML Infrastructure** | Foundation models, MLOps, AI hardware, edge AI | Fundamental enabling technology with expanding enterprise adoption |
| **Advanced Computing** | High-performance computing, cloud infrastructure, compute acceleration | Growing demand driven by AI workloads and data processing needs |
| **Semiconductor Design** | Chip design, EDA tools, specialty processors, photonics | Critical hardware layer with increasing design complexity |
| **Quantum Technologies** | Quantum computing, quantum sensing, quantum communications | Early-stage but transformational potential; key strategic technology |
| **Robotics & Automation** | Industrial robotics, autonomous systems, drone technology | Accelerating adoption driven by labour shortages and automation demand |
| **Advanced Materials** | Novel materials, nanotechnology, metamaterials | Foundational enabler across multiple deep-tech applications |
| **Synthetic Biology** | Bio-manufacturing, gene editing tools, computational biology | Rapidly advancing field with broad industrial applications |
| **Cleantech** | Energy storage, carbon capture, green hydrogen, climate tech | Growing urgency driven by climate commitments and policy support |

### 3.4 VCFM Qualifying Investment Compliance

All investments will be VCFM-qualifying:

- 100% of investments will be in securities directly issued by the portfolio company (primary issuance)
- All target companies will be unlisted at the time of investment
- All target companies will be incorporated for ≤10 years at initial investment
- The Fund will not invest in listed securities, IPOs, or secondary market transactions
- ≥80% of committed capital (net of fees and expenses) will be deployed in qualifying investments

## 7. RISK MANAGEMENT

### 7.1 Risk Management Framework

The Company's risk management framework addresses the following categories:

| Risk Category | Key Risks | Mitigation |
|---------------|-----------|------------|
| **Investment Risk** | Loss of capital; concentration risk; illiquidity | Diversified portfolio (15–25 companies); staged investment; IC oversight; reserves for follow-on |
| **Operational Risk** | Process failures; human error; system failures | Documented procedures; segregation of duties; BCP; IT resilience |
| **Compliance Risk** | Regulatory non-compliance; license conditions breach | Compliance Officer; ongoing monitoring; training; external advisory |
| **Market Risk** | Currency fluctuation; economic downturn | USD-denominated fund; long-term horizon (10-year fund life) |
| **Reputational Risk** | Negative press; portfolio company failures | Due diligence; active portfolio management; media monitoring |
| **Cyber Risk** | Data breaches; ransomware; phishing | Technology Risk Management Framework (see separate document) |
| **Legal Risk** | Contract disputes; regulatory actions | External legal counsel; proper documentation; insurance |
| **Key Person Risk** | Departure of key personnel | Key person provisions in LPA; succession planning; team development |
| **Counterparty Risk** | Fund admin/custodian failure | Due diligence; diversification of service providers; monitoring |

### 7.2 Valuation Policy

Portfolio investments will be valued in accordance with the International Private Equity and Venture Capital Valuation Guidelines ("IPEV Guidelines"):

- **Frequency:** Quarterly for reporting; annually for audited financial statements
- **Methodology:** Fair value based on the most appropriate valuation technique:
  - Recent transaction price (most common for early-stage)
  - Comparable company multiples (for growth-stage companies with revenue)
  - Discounted cash flow (where reliable projections are available)
  - Net asset value (for asset-heavy companies)
- **Independence:** Annual audit by independent auditor; LPAC review of valuation methodology
- **Write-downs:** Prompt recognition of impairment where indicators exist

### 7.3 Conflict of Interest Management

The Company has established policies to identify, prevent, and manage conflicts of interest:

- **Personal investment policy:** Directors and staff may not invest personally in portfolio companies without IC and Compliance Officer approval
- **Co-investment disclosure:** Any co-investment by the GP, affiliates, or staff alongside the Fund requires full LP disclosure
- **Cross-fund conflicts:** Not applicable (single fund initially); policy to be expanded if additional funds are raised
- **Allocation policy:** Not applicable (single fund initially)
- **Material conflicts:** Reported to Board and, where required, to the LP Advisory Committee
