## PART II — COMPANY STATUS AND OBJECTS

### 2. Name

2.1. The name of the Company is **Pefko Capital Management Pte. Ltd.**

### 3. Private Company

3.1. The Company is a private company limited by shares, and accordingly:

(a) the total number of Members shall not at any time exceed fifty (50), provided that where two or more persons hold one or more Shares jointly, they shall be treated as a single Member;

(b) no invitation shall be made to the public to subscribe for any Shares or debentures of the Company; and

(c) the right to transfer Shares shall be restricted as hereinafter provided.

### 4. Registered Office

4.1. The registered office of the Company shall be in the Republic of Singapore.

### 5. Objects and Powers

5.1. The principal activity of the Company is the management of venture capital funds as a licensed Venture Capital Fund Manager under the VCFM Regime administered by MAS.

5.2. Without limiting the generality of Clause 5.1, the objects for which the Company is established include:

(a) to carry on the business of fund management, including managing venture capital funds that invest primarily in securities directly issued by unlisted business ventures;

(b) to provide investment advisory and management services in connection with venture capital investments;

(c) to acquire, hold, manage, and dispose of investments for and on behalf of the funds managed by the Company;

(d) to enter into limited partnership agreements, subscription agreements, co-investment arrangements, and other fund documentation;

(e) to appoint and supervise fund administrators, custodians, auditors, legal counsel, and other service providers;

(f) to carry on any other business or activity which is incidental to or conducive to the attainment of any of the above objects;

(g) to comply with all applicable laws, regulations, guidelines, notices, and directions issued by MAS and any other regulatory authority; and

(h) to do all such other things as are incidental or conducive to the attainment of the objects set out above.

## PART III — SHARE CAPITAL

### 6. Authorised and Issued Share Capital

6.1. The share capital of the Company shall comprise ordinary shares with such rights and restrictions as may be prescribed by this Constitution or as may be determined by the Board from time to time.

6.2. The initial issued and paid-up share capital of the Company shall be S$[AMOUNT] divided into [NUMBER] ordinary shares of S$[PAR VALUE] each.

### 7. Variation of Rights

7.1. Subject to the Act, all or any of the rights attached to any class of shares may be varied with the consent in writing of the holders of not less than three-fourths of the issued shares of that class, or with the sanction of a Special Resolution passed at a separate General Meeting of the holders of shares of that class.

### 8. Issue of Shares

8.1. Subject to the Act and this Constitution, the unissued shares of the Company shall be at the disposal of the Directors who may allot and issue the same to such persons on such terms and conditions and at such times as the Directors think fit.

8.2. No shares shall be issued at a discount.

8.3. Every Member shall, on application, be entitled without payment to a certificate for the shares held by the Member.

### 9. Transfer of Shares

9.1. No Share shall be transferred to a person who is not a Member so long as any Member or any person selected by the Directors as one whom it is desirable in the interest of the Company to admit to membership is willing to purchase the Share at fair value.

9.2. The Directors may in their absolute discretion and without assigning any reason therefor decline to register any transfer of Shares to a person of whom they do not approve.

9.3. Subject to Clause 9.1 and 9.2, shares shall be transferable by instrument of transfer in any form approved by the Directors.

9.4. The instrument of transfer shall be signed by or on behalf of the transferor and, in the case of partly paid shares, by or on behalf of the transferee.

9.5. The Directors may require any transfer of Shares to be subject to prior written approval of MAS if such approval is required under the SFA or any applicable regulations.

### 10. Lien on Shares

10.1. The Company shall have a first and paramount lien on every Share (not being a fully paid Share) for all moneys (whether presently payable or not) called or payable at a fixed time in respect of that Share.

### 11. Call on Shares

11.1. The Directors may from time to time make calls upon the Members in respect of any moneys unpaid on their Shares, subject to the terms of issue.

### 12. Forfeiture of Shares

12.1. If a Member fails to pay any call or instalment on or before the day appointed for payment thereof, the Directors may serve a notice on the Member requiring payment, and in default may forfeit the Share.

## PART IV — GENERAL MEETINGS

### 13. Annual General Meeting

13.1. The Company shall hold an Annual General Meeting in each year in accordance with the Act.

13.2. The Annual General Meeting shall be held at such time and place as the Directors shall appoint.

### 14. Extraordinary General Meeting

14.1. All General Meetings other than Annual General Meetings shall be called Extraordinary General Meetings.

14.2. The Directors may, whenever they think fit, convene an Extraordinary General Meeting.

14.3. An Extraordinary General Meeting shall also be convened on the requisition of Members holding not less than 10% of the total number of paid-up shares carrying the right to vote at General Meetings.

### 15. Notice of General Meetings

15.1. At least 14 days' notice shall be given for a General Meeting. The notice shall specify the place, day, and hour of the meeting and the general nature of the business to be transacted.

15.2. A General Meeting may be called by shorter notice if consented to by:
(a) for an Annual General Meeting: all Members entitled to attend and vote; and
(b) for any other meeting: a majority in number of the Members having a right to attend and vote, being a majority holding not less than 95% of the total voting rights.

### 16. Quorum

16.1. No business shall be transacted at any General Meeting unless a quorum of Members is present at the time when the meeting proceeds to business.

16.2. Two Members present in person or by proxy shall be a quorum. If the Company has only one Member, one Member present in person or by proxy shall constitute a quorum.

### 17. Voting

17.1. Subject to any rights or restrictions attached to any class of shares, every Member present in person or by proxy shall have one vote on a show of hands and one vote for each share held on a poll.

17.2. A resolution put to the vote of the meeting shall be decided on a show of hands unless a poll is demanded.

17.3. A Member may appoint a proxy to attend and vote at any General Meeting. The instrument appointing a proxy shall be in writing.

### 18. Written Resolutions

18.1. Subject to the Act, a resolution in writing signed by all Members entitled to vote shall be as valid and effective as if the same had been passed at a General Meeting duly convened and held.

## PART V — DIRECTORS

### 19. Number and Appointment of Directors

19.1. The Company shall have not less than two (2) Directors at all times.

19.2. At least one Director shall be ordinarily resident in Singapore, as required by the Act.

19.3. At least one Director shall serve as a full-time employee of the Company.

19.4. Directors shall be appointed by Ordinary Resolution at a General Meeting, or by the Board to fill a casual vacancy, subject to retirement and re-election at the next Annual General Meeting.

### 20. Fit and Proper Requirements

20.1. All Directors must satisfy the fit and proper criteria as prescribed by MAS under the Guidelines on Fit and Proper Criteria [FSG-G01] and any other applicable guidelines.

20.2. No person shall be appointed as a Director if such appointment would cause the Company to fail to comply with any requirement imposed by MAS.

20.3. The Company shall notify MAS of any change in its directors within 14 business days or such period as required by MAS.

### 21. Powers of Directors

21.1. The business and affairs of the Company shall be managed by or under the direction of the Board.

21.2. The Board may exercise all the powers of the Company except those required by the Act or this Constitution to be exercised by the Company in General Meeting.

21.3. Without prejudice to the general powers conferred by this Constitution and the Act, the Directors shall have the following specific powers:

(a) to manage the day-to-day operations of the Company, including investment decisions for the funds managed by the Company;

(b) to appoint and remove the CEO, Responsible Officers, and other key personnel of the Company subject to MAS requirements;

(c) to establish investment committees, compliance committees, and other committees as necessary;

(d) to execute, on behalf of the Company, all fund documentation including limited partnership agreements, subscription agreements, and side letters;

(e) to ensure compliance with all MAS regulatory requirements, including the VCFM licensing conditions;

(f) to approve the Company's compliance policies, AML/CFT framework, and technology risk management framework; and

(g) to delegate any of their powers to committees, individual Directors, or officers of the Company.

### 22. Meetings of Directors

22.1. The Directors may meet together for the dispatch of business, adjourn, and otherwise regulate their meetings as they think fit.

22.2. A Director may at any time convene a meeting of the Directors.

22.3. The quorum necessary for the transaction of business of the Directors shall be two (2) Directors present in person, by telephone, or by video conference.

22.4. Questions arising at any meeting shall be decided by a majority of votes.

22.5. A resolution in writing signed by all the Directors shall be as valid and effectual as if it had been passed at a meeting of Directors duly convened and held.

### 23. Disclosure of Interests

23.1. A Director who is in any way, whether directly or indirectly, interested in a transaction or proposed transaction with the Company shall declare the nature and extent of that interest in accordance with the Act.

### 24. Remuneration of Directors

24.1. The remuneration of the Directors shall be determined by the Company in General Meeting. The Directors may be paid all travelling, hotel, and other expenses properly incurred in attending meetings or in connection with the business of the Company.

### 25. Removal and Vacation of Office

25.1. The Company may by Ordinary Resolution remove a Director before the expiration of the Director's period of office.

25.2. The office of a Director shall be vacated if the Director:

(a) becomes bankrupt or makes any arrangement or composition with creditors;
(b) becomes of unsound mind;
(c) resigns by notice in writing to the Company;
(d) is absent from Directors' meetings for more than six months without the permission of the Board;
(e) ceases to satisfy the fit and proper requirements prescribed by MAS; or
(f) is removed by Ordinary Resolution of the Company.

## PART VI — CHIEF EXECUTIVE OFFICER AND RESPONSIBLE OFFICERS

### 26. Chief Executive Officer

26.1. The Board shall appoint a Chief Executive Officer ("CEO") who shall be responsible for the day-to-day management and operations of the Company.

26.2. The CEO must be ordinarily resident in Singapore and satisfy the fit and proper criteria prescribed by MAS.

26.3. The CEO may concurrently hold other roles within the Company, including Executive Director, relevant professional, and MAS representative, as permitted under the VCFM Regime.

### 27. Responsible Officers

27.1. The Company shall at all times maintain a minimum of two (2) Responsible Officers as required by MAS for the VCFM Regime.

27.2. Each Responsible Officer must be ordinarily resident in Singapore and satisfy the fit and proper criteria prescribed by MAS.

27.3. The Board shall ensure that all Responsible Officers are duly notified to MAS and that any changes are reported within the prescribed timeframes.

---

## PART VII — SECRETARY

### 28. Company Secretary

28.1. The Company shall have at least one Secretary who shall be a natural person ordinarily resident in Singapore.

28.2. The Secretary shall be appointed by the Directors and shall hold office on such terms as the Directors determine.

---

## PART VIII — DIVIDENDS AND RESERVES

### 29. Dividends

29.1. The Company in General Meeting may declare dividends, but no dividend shall exceed the amount recommended by the Directors.

29.2. The Directors may pay interim dividends if the financial position of the Company justifies such payment.

29.3. No dividend shall be paid otherwise than out of profits.

### 30. Reserves

30.1. The Directors may set aside out of the profits of the Company such sums as they think proper as reserves, which shall be applicable for any purpose to which the profits of the Company may properly be applied.

---

## PART IX — ACCOUNTS AND AUDIT

### 31. Accounts

31.1. The Directors shall cause proper books of account to be kept in accordance with the Act and all applicable accounting standards.

31.2. The financial year of the Company shall end on [31 December / 31 March] of each year, or such other date as the Directors may determine.

### 32. Audit

32.1. The Company shall appoint auditors in accordance with the Act, unless the Company qualifies for and elects the small company audit exemption under Section 205C of the Act.

32.2. Notwithstanding any audit exemption, the Company shall maintain such financial reporting standards as required by MAS for VCFM license holders, including the filing of the Annual Declaration (Form 25A) within one month of the end of each financial year.

---

## PART X — REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

### 33. MAS Regulatory Requirements

33.1. The Company shall at all times comply with:

(a) the conditions of its VCFM license issued by MAS;
(b) the Securities and Futures Act 2001 and all subsidiary legislation;
(c) all applicable MAS notices, guidelines, and directions, including but not limited to:
   - Notice SFA 04-N02 (AML/CFT);
   - Guidelines SFA 04-G05 (Licensing and Conduct of Business for Fund Management Companies);
   - MAS Technology Risk Management Guidelines;
   - Guidelines on Fit and Proper Criteria [FSG-G01]; and
(d) any other applicable Written Law.

### 34. Restriction on Activities

34.1. The Company shall only manage funds that qualify as venture capital funds under the VCFM Regime, being funds that invest at least 80% of committed capital (excluding fees and expenses) in securities directly issued by unlisted business ventures incorporated for no more than 10 years at the time of the fund's initial investment.

34.2. The Company shall not undertake any regulated activity for which it does not hold the requisite license or exemption.

### 35. MAS Approvals for Changes

35.1. Prior written approval of MAS shall be obtained before any change in:

(a) the substantial shareholders of the Company (being any person with an interest of 20% or more of the total number of issued shares);
(b) the CEO or any Responsible Officer; or
(c) any matter for which MAS approval is required under the SFA or the conditions of the VCFM license.

---

## PART XI — INDEMNITY AND INSURANCE

### 36. Indemnity

36.1. Subject to the Act, every Director or officer of the Company shall be indemnified by the Company against all costs, charges, losses, expenses, and liabilities incurred in the execution of their duties, except such as are incurred through their own negligence, wilful default, breach of duty, or breach of trust.

### 37. Insurance

37.1. The Company may purchase and maintain insurance for any Director or officer against any liability incurred in that capacity, to the extent permitted by law.

---

## PART XII — WINDING UP

### 38. Distribution on Winding Up

38.1. If the Company shall be wound up, the assets available for distribution among the Members shall be distributed to the Members in proportion to the number of shares held by them respectively, subject to the rights of any shares issued on special conditions.

### 39. MAS Notification

39.1. The Company shall notify MAS of any resolution for voluntary winding up and shall comply with all MAS requirements relating to the surrender of the VCFM license.

---

## PART XIII — GENERAL PROVISIONS

### 40. Notices

40.1. A notice may be given by the Company to any Member by sending it by post to the Member's registered address, or by electronic means with the Member's consent.

### 41. Governing Law
